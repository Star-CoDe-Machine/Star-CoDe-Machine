Rabindranath Tagore

the Bard of Bengal

Here's a time line of Rabindranath Tagore's life:

  • 1861-Rabindranath Tagore was born on May 7, in Calcutta (now Kolkata), India.
  • 1877-Published his first substantial poems under the pseudonym "Bhānusiṃha" ("Sun Lion").
  • 1882-Published "Sandhya Sangit" (Evening Songs), a collection of poems.
  • 1890-Published the novel "Manasi," marking the beginning of his prominence in Bengali literature.
  • 1901-Founded the experimental school Santiniketan, where he sought to blend the best of Indian and Western traditions.
  • 1910-Published "Gitanjali" (Song Offerings), a collection of poems that gained him worldwide recognition.
  • 1931- Completed "The Religion of Man," a collection of his lectures at Oxford University.
  • 1940- Delivered the Hibbert Lecture series at the University of Oxford.

"Rabindranath Tagore's words and ideas have transcended time and space, inspiring countless minds to dream, create, and aspire for a better world.

--A.P.J. Abdul Kalam
